Styles of Painting

Styles

  1. Prehistoric
    The representation of humans in cave paintings was rare. Mostly, animals were painted, not only animals that were used as food but also animals that represented strength.
  2. Egyptian
    A civilization with very strong traditions of architecture and sculpture.
  3. Greek and Roman
    Had skilled painters, sculptors (though both endeavours were regarded as mere manual labour at the time), and architects.
  4. Gothic
  5. Renaissance
  6. Modern Art

  1. Impressionism
    Impressionist paintings demonstrated that human beings do not see objects, but instead see light itself.
  2. Abstract
    Uses a visual language of form, color, and line to create a composition that may exist with a degree of independence from visual references in the world.
